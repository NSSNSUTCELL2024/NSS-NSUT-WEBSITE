Harsh Dhaia Sir

Former Administrative Coordinator

My journey with the National Service Scheme (NSS) at college has been an enriching experience, offering me a chance to engage in a diverse range of community service activities. It all began with on-ground work at the National Integration Camp (NIC) during the summers when college is mostly deserted during the holidays. As a modest volunteer, I created lasting memories, developed friendships with volunteers from across India, and experienced sleepless nights filled with hard work and camaraderie. Post NIC, I was entrusted with the role of Administrative Coordinator, which allowed me to organize impactful events such as the "Run for Unity" in collaboration with the DM office. Late-night work at the Student Activity Centre and enduring the chilling winters, complete with bonfires, during a special camp at the University Adopted Village on Delhi's outskirts were unforgettable. Through NSS, I made diverse friends from various cultures and learned invaluable lessons in leadership and teamwork. In my final year, I had the privilege of leading the design team for NSS’s Azad magazine, a fulfilling creative endeavor. I wish the NSS unit continued success and hope it reaches new heights, setting even higher benchmarks in community service.
